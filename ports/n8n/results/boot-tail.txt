===== INTERPRETATION =====
Of the ~3.7 s of network wait that 80 ms RTT injects into stock n8n boot:

  data-fetch wait (/rest + /types)     520 ms   14%   SERVER-PRE-COMPLETABLE
  asset (JS bundle) download wait     3200 ms   86%   NOT hideable (client needs the bundle)
  FCP                          2764 -> 6232 ms         (+3.5 s, ~all asset download)

n8n boot is BUNDLE-DOWNLOAD-bound, not data-wait-bound. 238 JS chunks over HTTP/1.1's
~6 connections, each paying an RTT, is the villain.

Ceiling for the "server boots the app / ships the continuation, client adopts" idea on
n8n = ~520 ms (14%). The dominant 3.2 s is asset download that survives any data-preboot,
because the client still runs the app locally after adoption. This is also the ceiling
for Remix loaders / Qwik / RSC — anything that pre-fetches DATA. No preloader wins much
on n8n's boot.

Levers that WOULD move n8n boot (orthogonal to tierless): HTTP/2 multiplexing (collapse
the 238-request-over-6-conns penalty), fewer/larger chunks, modulepreload, per-tier
dead-code shake (ROADMAP). Caveats: 238 reqs/HTTP-1.1 is partly a preview-server
artifact (prod HTTP/2 + CDN cuts asset wait, still > 520 ms); the data path shows ~2-3
layers of depth (520 ms / 12 reqs, not flat fan-out), so migration could collapse
~300-500 ms — small, but the only genuinely tierless-shaped boot win here.

CONCLUSION: n8n's boot is the wrong battle for a network-wait win — for tierless OR any
preloading framework. Confirms the corpus claim must be distributional: preloading/
migration pay where DATA wait or dependency DEPTH dominate, not where asset download and
compute do (n8n).
